King Crab Legs

How To Make it:

To make king crab legs, start by bringing a large pot of water to a boil. Add salt and any desired seasonings like Old Bay seasoning or lemon slices to enhance the flavor. Carefully place the crab legs in the boiling water, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cook for about 6-8 minutes or until the crab legs are heated through. Using tongs, remove the crab legs from the pot and let them drain briefly. Serve the succulent king crab legs with melted butter and lemon wedges for dipping. Enjoy the tender and delicious meat by cracking the shells and extracting the meat using a crab cracker or kitchen scissors.

Ingredients:

  • King crab legs
  • Water (enough to fully submerge the crab legs)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Seasonings (optional, such as Old Bay seasoning, lemon slices, or garlic powder)
  • Melted butter (for serving)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Steps:

  1. Fill a large pot with enough water to fully submerge the king crab legs. Place the pot on the stove over high heat and bring the water to a boil.
  2. Once the water is boiling, add salt to the pot. The amount of salt can vary depending on your taste preferences, but a good starting point is around 1-2 tablespoons of salt for every gallon of water.
  3. If desired, add seasonings to the pot to enhance the flavor of the crab legs. Popular choices include Old Bay seasoning, lemon slices, garlic powder, or any other spices you enjoy. Stir the seasonings into the boiling water.
  4. Carefully place the king crab legs into the pot, making sure they are fully submerged in the boiling water. You may need to bend or break the crab legs slightly to fit them into the pot, if necessary.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium-high and let the crab legs cook for about 6-8 minutes. The exact cooking time may vary depending on the size of the crab legs, so adjust accordingly. The crab legs are ready when they are heated through.
  6. While the crab legs are cooking, prepare a serving dish and melt some butter. You can do this by microwaving the butter in short intervals or by using a small saucepan on low heat.
  7. Once the crab legs are cooked, carefully remove them from the pot using tongs, ensuring that excess water drains off briefly.
  8. Transfer the cooked king crab legs to the serving dish and serve them with melted butter and lemon wedges on the side.
  9. To eat the crab legs, use a crab cracker or kitchen scissors to crack the shells. Extract the meat from the legs and enjoy the succulent and flavorful king crab meat.
  10. Dip the crab meat in melted butter and squeeze fresh lemon juice over it for added taste. Serve any additional sides or accompaniments of your choice, and savor the delicious king crab legs.
